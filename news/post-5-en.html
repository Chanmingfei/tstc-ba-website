Announcement 2026-07-15 TSNU Bar Mod Team

TSNU Bar Network-Wide Anti-Fraud Alert

To all TSNU students, incoming Class of 2026, and parents,

We are now at a critical stage of admissions inquiries and new-student preparation, when all kinds of campus and admissions scams surge. Recently, this bar has repeatedly found unfamiliar out-of-province IP accounts impersonating our senior students and current students, using the pretext of campus Q&A and enrollment guidance to privately sell goods or charge fees for proxy services. Such fraud seriously disrupts the order of the bar and harms students' property safety and legitimate rights.

To eliminate these fraudulent practices and protect the personal, property, and information security of all members, and to maintain a pure, legitimate, and safe communication environment on the TSNU Bar, the Mod Team hereby issues this network-wide anti-fraud alert, clarifying common scam tactics, prevention rules, and platform management regulations. Please read it carefully, share it with others, and strictly comply.

I. High-Risk Scam Tactics (Everyone Must Guard Against)

1. Out-of-Province IPs Impersonating Seniors

Numerous unfamiliar out-of-province IP accounts, not based in Tangshan, disguise themselves as our senior students, student leaders, or campus volunteers, and proactively message freshmen and lower-grade students, gaining trust through claims of "one-on-one enrollment guidance," "internal campus info," or "sharing experience from those who've been there." These accounts have no verified school identity and no real campus activity; their sole purpose is traffic, profit, and fraud. They are the epicenter of recent campus scams—highly covert and with a very high victim rate.

2. Selling Counterfeit Goods Under the Guise of Seniors

Relying on their fake identities, scammers use bait like "campus-exclusive discounts," "must-have freshman supplies," or "group-buy deals" to privately peddle military-training uniforms, bedding and mattresses, campus phone cards, study materials, storage items, CET-4/6 materials, and postgraduate entrance exam papers. The goods sold are mostly low-quality, unbranded, or pirated, with no after-sales service or guarantee; once paid, the sellers block and ghost you. This is a typical campus scam.

3. Fake Admissions Consulting and Paid Proxy Services

All unofficial accounts, under the name of the school or posing as seniors, falsely claim they can handle enrollment procedures, dorm reassignments, major consultations, course selection guidance, or club pre-arrangements, luring private transfers under names like "service fee," "expedite fee," "proxy fee," or "deposit." We solemnly state: all school processes—admissions, registration, dorm assignment, and major transfer—have no private proxy services and no extra charges whatsoever.

4. Fake Freshman Group Recruitment Scams

Scammers privately create various unofficial "TSNU freshman groups, Q&A groups, benefit groups," impersonate administrators to post false notices and fake benefits, promote products in the groups, drive traffic for profit, and harvest freshmen's private information such as names, phone numbers, admission details, and family information—later committing targeted fraud or illegally reselling data.

II. Official Solemn Statements

1. The School Has No Private Admissions Agents

The TSNU Admissions Office and all departments have never authorized any off-campus personnel, current students, online influencers, or bar users to conduct admissions consulting, paid proxy services, or goods sales. All fee-based services in a private name are fraud.

2. School Students Do Not Coerce Sales

Legitimate enrolled students will not, through unfamiliar private messages or out-of-province IP accounts, privately coerce or induce freshmen to buy supplies or sign up for paid services. All private transactions are unofficial.

3. The Only Official Information Channels

All authoritative information—admissions policies, enrollment guidelines, registration procedures, dorm arrangements, and fee standards—is released only through the TSNU official website, the official admissions WeChat account, and notices from the school Admissions Office. This bar is merely a communication platform; all unofficial statements are for reference only. Do not trust and transfer money based on them.

III. Bar Management Penalty Rules

To root out fraud, the Mod Team will launch a comprehensive anti-fraud rectification campaign and deal strictly with violating accounts:

1. Strict Crackdown on Impersonation Accounts

We will strictly investigate out-of-province IPs impersonating our seniors and fake Q&A/traffic-driving accounts. Once verified, they will be permanently banned and blocked, and their posts deleted.

2. Private Sales and Promotion Strictly Prohibited

All private accounts are strictly prohibited from selling campus supplies, promoting phone cards, peddling materials, or offering paid proxy services within this bar or via private messages. Violators will be permanently muted and banned.

3. Unauthorized Traffic Redirection Strictly Prohibited

Publicly posting unofficial freshman groups, traffic-driving QR codes, or private contact info is strictly prohibited, to eliminate all scam-traffic behavior.

4. Illegal Acts Referred to Authorities

For accounts that spread rumors, run fake admissions schemes, induce transfers, or defraud others of property, the Mod Team will, in addition to platform bans, preserve evidence and, when necessary, refer the case to the school security office and public security authorities.

IV. Anti-Fraud Precautions for Everyone

1. Refuse Private Transactions

Do not add strangers who message you privately, and do not believe in "senior-exclusive deals." For all daily and study supplies, prefer official platforms and on-campus stores. Firmly reject private transfers and offline private deals.

2. Verify Account Identity

When encountering "seniors" who proactively offer guidance or push products, be sure to verify their real identity. Out-of-province unfamiliar IPs, accounts with no campus activity, and newly registered small accounts should all be treated as high-risk scam accounts—block and report them immediately.

3. Protect Personal Privacy

Never disclose admission details, ID numbers, phone numbers, home addresses, bank card information, or any verification codes to strangers, to prevent information leaks that lead to secondary fraud.

4. Verify Through Multiple Channels

For any questions involving payments, proxy services, or enrollment policies, first check the school's official channels or consult your counselor or official teachers. Do not trust strangers online.

5. Report Promptly

If you encounter scam-traffic, fake sales, or identity impersonation, take screenshots as evidence promptly and report them via private message to the Mod Team, so we can work together to combat fraud.

Anti-fraud is everyone's responsibility! We hope all TSNU students stay vigilant, keep your eyes open, abandon any fluke mentality, and stay away from all campus cyber scams. Let us jointly maintain a safe, civilized, and clean campus online environment!

TSNU Bar Mod Team
