A Friendly Reminder on Posting Etiquette in the Bar

Dear fellow bar members, the TSNU Bar is our shared online space. So that every student can communicate here with peace of mind and speak freely, the Mod Team has put together a small reminder about posting etiquette. We kindly ask that you keep it in mind when posting and commenting.

1. Be Gentle with One Another

On the other end of the network are real people. We encourage the clash of ideas, but we reject unfriendly speech such as personal attacks, regional discrimination, and gender antagonism. A word of "I understand how you feel" is often more powerful than a rebuttal. For suggestions regarding the school's work, we recommend first giving feedback to your counselor or department leadership; the Mod Team members are fellow students who can relate to your concerns—please do not post dangerous remarks in the Bar that could bring trouble upon yourself, as rational feedback is more likely to resolve issues.

2. Please Do Not Post This Information

  • Others' real names, phone numbers, home addresses, and other personal privacy;
  • Unverified rumors and misinformation;
  • Advertising, click-farming, part-time job solicitation, and other marketing content;
  • Images or text that violate laws, regulations, or platform rules.

3. What to Do When You Encounter Violations

If you come across inappropriate content, there is no need to argue in person—you can click "Report" at the bottom right of the post, or directly @ the on-duty junior moderator. We will handle it as soon as possible and protect the privacy of the reporter.

Civil posting begins with three seconds of thought before every "Send". Thank you for making this Bar feel more like a home.

4. On Post Deletion and Appeals

All actions by the Mod Team follow the principles of transparency and prudence. If you believe a post was deleted by mistake, you are welcome to explain the situation through the "Feedback" section on the homepage or via the moderator mailbox, and we will carefully review and respond.

May our Bar remain bright, warm, and trustworthy.
