Freshman Guide (4) - Dormitory Life Information

The dormitory is the primary living space throughout your four years of university. This article compiles the real rules on electricity use, access control, facilities, repairs, and bathhouses across both campuses, along with key warnings about the common sales scams that spike at the start of term.

Everything here is based on verified on-campus research. Read together with the earlier freshman guides, it should help you quickly adapt to dormitory life on campus.

I. Dormitory Power Supply and Electricity Rules

Dormitory power is supplied on a fixed schedule from 6:30 a.m. to 11:30 p.m.

The university prohibits all high-power appliances and electric heating devices. Although there are no routine inspections or confiscations of prohibited items, using them will trip the dormitory power directly. Once tripped, power cannot be restored on your own—you must contact the dorm supervisor, which is a hassle, so we don't recommend taking the risk.

II. Dormitory Access Control

The dormitory evening access-control curfew is at 11:00 p.m.

Returning after 11:00 p.m. counts as a late return and requires you to proactively register your information with the dorm supervisor. Try to return on time to avoid frequent late-return registrations.

III. Bed-Curtain Usage

The university nominally prohibits hanging bed curtains in dormitories.

During your freshman year there are regular dormitory inspections with relatively strict control; after sophomore year inspections essentially stop and restrictions loosen, so students who want to install bed curtains can set them up later.

IV. Floor Public Facilities

Both sides of every dormitory floor are equipped with public water dispensers and public washing machines.

  • Water dispenser: supports both QR-code scanning and facial-recognition for water access;
  • Washing machine: usable by QR-code scan, meeting daily drinking-water and laundry needs.

V. Dormitory Repair Channels

For damaged dormitory facilities such as lights, door locks, faucets, or sockets, there are two ways to report a repair:

  • Go to the dorm supervisor's office downstairs to register a repair request;
  • Call the university's official repair hotline directly: 0315-3863211.

VI. On-Campus Bathhouse Locations

None of the dormitory buildings have private bathrooms; you must go to the on-campus public bathhouse to shower.

  • Daxuedao Campus: currently only the public bathhouse east of the North Courtyard cafeteria is open; the South Courtyard public bathhouse is under renovation and is expected to open after the start of the fall 2026 semester;
  • Xueyuan Road Campus: the public bathhouse is located near the sports and arts center.

VII. Dormitory Shopping and Parcel Delivery

  • Shopping: the on-campus mini-mart has limited selection; we don't recommend buying bulky daily necessities on campus—you can shop at off-campus supermarkets or order online in advance;
  • Parcels (Daxuedao Campus): there is no on-campus parcel station; all parcels are picked up at the parcel point on the lower level of the Yulania Hotel, west of the North Courtyard.

VIII. Key Warning for Freshmen: Start-of-Term Door-to-Door Sales Scams

The days just before the semester starts are a peak period for dormitory sales scams. At this time, freshmen's access-control information has not yet been fully entered into the system, so campus access control is looser, and many off-campus people take the chance to slip into dormitory buildings to peddle door to door.

Whether the other party is selling courses, books, haircut cards, or daily necessities, do not trust them and do not buy anything.

Key warning: anyone who claims to be from "this school's Marketing program" and comes to your dorm selling pens is an off-campus fraudster. Our school has no Marketing program; their stationery is wildly overpriced and of terrible quality—a common scam targeting new students. Just refuse directly.

IX. General Life Tips

  • Keep your dormitory clean and tidy daily; the school conducts regular hygiene inspections;
  • Try to be tolerant of roommates' schedules to maintain a good dormitory atmosphere;
  • For rules on the campus card and on-campus network resources, refer to the first freshman guide in this series.
