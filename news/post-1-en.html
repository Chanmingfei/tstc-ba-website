Summary 2025-07-02 TSNU Bar Mod Team

TSNU Bar 2025 Spring Semester Work Summary

As the semester comes to a close, the Mod Team would like to offer a plain and honest summary to report our work to every bar member and hear your thoughts. The bar is no one's "private plot"—it is our shared public space.

I. Content Development

This semester we kept curating campus news, exam schedules, and event previews, and tried to present them with clearer formatting. We also launched the "News & Updates" section, gathering what used to be scattered announcements into a retrievable timeline.

  • Published several official announcements and friendly reminders;
  • Organized practical posts such as freshman guides and course-selection tips;
  • Pinned frequently asked questions to reduce duplicate inquiries.

II. Event Organization

In line with the school calendar, we organized online Q&A sessions and experience sharing during events such as freshman welcome and exam seasons. Although most were carried out quietly, every interaction brought us closer to our fellow students.

III. Community Moderation

This semester we handled several cases of violating content, mainly through reminders and post deletion, with bans as a supplementary measure. We have always upheld "education first, cautious punishment" and kept an appeal channel open.

The purpose of moderation is not control, but to let more people dare to—and enjoy—speaking up here.

IV. Shortcomings & Outlook

We are also aware that our response speed and content quality still have room for improvement; some features (such as on-site search and a submission portal) are not yet complete. Next semester we plan to:

  1. Establish more transparent post-deletion and appeal guidelines;
  2. Open submissions to bar members to enrich our content;
  3. Optimize "News & Updates" to support category-based search.

Thank you for your company this semester. Next semester, we will continue to build the bar into a trustworthy corner of campus life.
