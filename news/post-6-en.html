Freshman Guide (1) — Is the Campus Card Worth Getting?

Dear new students:

Around enrollment, many people promote campus phone cards. Quite a few new students cannot tell official channels from scams, and are unsure whether getting one is necessary. This article objectively and comprehensively explains all the facts about the campus Unicom card. The school does not require anyone to get a card, so you can decide for yourself based on your situation.

I. Use the Only Official Channel and Beware of Sales Scams

1. Official Authorized Outlets

Only the on-campus Unicom service hall is the official partner outlet that can issue the campus card enabling dormitory-exclusive WiFi. Plan contracts and pricing rules are fully transparent, and you can review all terms on the spot.

2. Watch Out for Scammers Posing as Senior Students

Recently, scams have been frequent in the Tieba forum and new-student groups: accounts from other provinces with unfamiliar IPs, off-campus people pretending to be seniors or orientation volunteers, contacting students via private messages or going door-to-door in dormitories to sell phone cards.

  • These people have no real affiliation with the school; their plans hide misleading fine print, overstate data allowances, and offer no after-sales support.
  • Phone cards sold privately (not at the service hall) cannot activate the dormitory WiFi, and after you pay you can easily be blocked and left unable to reach the seller.
  • Any promotion that is not handled on-site at the on-campus Unicom service hall and instead asks you to transfer money privately online is suspicious fraud — do not trust it.

II. Full Details on the Campus Unicom Card's Real Pricing and Network Access

1. Fixed Pricing

The Unicom campus card here has a flat rate of 39 yuan/month, with no gimmicks such as long-term discounts or hidden offers. Each month includes 20GB of nationwide data and 260GB of in-province data; if unused, up to 20GB of nationwide data and 140GB of in-province data can roll over to the next month. That is indeed a large amount, roughly equivalent to unlimited data, but to be objective: this monthly fee is not cheap among student data plans.

2. Dormitory WiFi Usage Rules

The campus has exclusive Unicom WiFi in the teaching buildings, library, and dormitory areas; access to this WiFi is tied to the campus Unicom card:

  • When the card is properly activated and not in arrears, you can connect directly to the dormitory WiFi with a stable connection.
  • Without a campus card, or if the card is suspended for non-payment, you cannot connect to the dormitory’s exclusive WiFi.

3. Supplementary Note on Accessing On-Campus Learning Resources (Key Fact)

Many people mislead freshmen by claiming 'you can't access on-campus resources without a campus card' — this is false: the school provides a free official VPN service to all enrolled students. You can log in with your student ID and unified identity account. Whether or not you have a Unicom campus card, as long as your phone or computer has any other internet connection, you can connect to the school's free VPN and normally access all internal resources such as the library databases and the academic affairs system, with no resource restrictions.

III. Reference Advice on Getting a Campus Card: Entirely Voluntary, Choose as Needed

The school and individual colleges will not force any student to get a campus card; simply decide based on your current phone plan and internet needs:

When Getting a Campus Card Is Recommended

  1. You spend long hours online in the dorm watching shows, attending online classes, or downloading files, and need stable dorm WiFi.
  2. Your current plan costs more than 39 yuan/month with little data, and no better-value option is available.
  3. You would rather not log into the VPN manually every time you look up papers or use the academic system, and prefer the convenience of one-tap direct WiFi access.

When Getting a Campus Card Is Not Recommended

  1. You already have a cheaper plan with plenty of data and only use the internet lightly.
  2. Your dorm internet needs are minimal, and you are willing to use the school’s free VPN to access on-campus resources.
  3. You do not want the fixed 39 yuan/month cost long-term and have a limited budget.

IV. Pitfall Summary for New Students Getting a Card

  1. Only go to the on-campus Unicom service hall to get a card; reject door-to-door dorm visits and online promotions from out-of-province accounts.
  2. The 39 yuan/month plan is not competitively priced — don’t follow the crowd and sign up blindly.
  3. Distinguish the two types of network access: dorm WiFi requires the campus card, but on-campus learning resources can be reached via the free VPN whether or not you have a card — don’t be misled by false claims.
  4. Any claim that "you can't look up papers or use the academic system without a campus card" is false advertising. If you encounter a suspicious promoter, promptly notify your counselor, the dorm manager, or the campus security office.
  5. If you fall victim to a sales scam, keep screenshots of the chats and transfers, and contact the Tieba moderators or the school’s network information center to report it.
