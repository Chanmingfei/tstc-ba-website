Freshman Guide (7): How to Find Classrooms at Daxuedao Campus

Few things are more stressful on your first day of class than staring at a room number on your schedule and not knowing which building it's in. Daxuedao Campus is split into a North Courtyard and a South Courtyard, each with its own teaching buildings and room-numbering rules. This guide organizes the main teaching buildings, numbering conventions, and practical wayfinding tips so you can check it anytime after the semester starts and avoid getting lost or arriving late.

I. North Courtyard Teaching Buildings

The North Courtyard has three teaching buildings, and most humanities courses are scheduled in this area.

1. Humanities Complex (North Courtyard Main Building)

This is the core classroom building for humanities departments, with staircase classrooms on both sides. Room numbers throughout the building start with "Wen" (文). Example regular classroom: Wen 211; example lecture hall: Wen 8 Lecture Hall.

The building has elevators, but waits can be long during class-change peaks. If your classroom is on a higher floor, leave early and consider taking the stairs to avoid being late.

2. Laboratory Building

Houses experimental equipment and is reserved for science practical courses; regular theory classes are not held here.

3. Information Building

The main computer labs are concentrated here; computer fundamentals and software practice courses are held here.

II. South Courtyard Teaching Buildings

The South Courtyard has more teaching buildings and is the main area for science departments, including regular teaching buildings, arts-only buildings, and the Jingbo Building.

1. Buildings 2, 3, 4, and 5 (Regular Teaching Buildings)

Mainly used for science courses (Building 3 has microteaching classrooms for teacher-training students). Room numbers follow the pattern building number + floor + room, e.g. 3101 means Room 1 on the first floor of Building 3.

2. Building 6

The entire building consists of combined-class lecture halls used for large public courses and multi-class lectures. Rooms are named "Combined Classroom (number)," e.g. Combined Classroom (9).

3. Music Building

Dedicated to music majors, with practice rooms and rehearsal halls; non-arts majors generally have no classes here.

4. Fine Arts Building

Exclusive to the Fine Arts College, with professional studios and exhibition halls; only art-related courses are held here.

5. Jingbo Building

Contains computer labs, a teacher-skills training center, and a counseling room; the Putonghua (Mandarin) proficiency test is held here. Room numbers use the building name plus room number, e.g. Jingbo 302.

III. General Classroom-Finding Tips

  1. Quickly tell north from south: Room numbers containing "Wen" belong to the Humanities Complex in the North Courtyard; pure numbers, "Combined Classroom," or "Jingbo" are all in the South Courtyard.
  2. Leave early for upper floors: Except for the Humanities Complex in the North Courtyard, other teaching buildings have no elevators, so leave early for higher-floor classrooms.
  3. Empty classrooms for self-study: Unoccupied classrooms are open for self-study. Main corridors are crowded between classes, so travel off-peak when possible.
  4. Scope: This guide covers Daxuedao Campus only; a separate guide for Xueyuan Road Campus buildings will be published later.
