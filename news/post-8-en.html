Freshman Guide (3) - What PE Courses Are Available and How to Choose

PE course selection at the start of the semester is one of the things freshmen care about most. Our school offers two selection modes — offline on-site selection and online system selection — each with its own special rules and procedures.

Based on real on-campus experience, below is the complete selection process, course venues, and practical advice to help you prepare in advance.

I. Two PE Course Selection Modes

After enrollment, PE course selection goes through two stages: offline selection before your information is entered into the academic system, and online system selection after it is entered.

1. Offline On-Field Selection

When freshmen first arrive and their personal information has not yet been entered into the academic system, the school uses offline selection uniformly.

  • All classes scheduled at the same time gather together at the playground, where the supervising teacher arranges the groups and announces each PE teacher's assigned spot;
  • After the teacher gives the signal, all students run simultaneously to their preferred teacher's position;
  • Each teacher creates a course group on the spot, and spots are filled by joining the group — once the group is full, the course is full.

This method mainly tests your running speed and how fast you join the group. If you are slow, you may miss your preferred course, so decide your target in advance and take a favorable position.

2. Online Selection via the Academic System

Once enrollment information is entered, PE selection switches entirely to online self-service selection.

Log into the school's academic system, go to the course selection section, and choose "Self-Service Selection." During the open window, grab a spot relying on your connection speed and quick hands — popular courses fill up almost instantly, so log in early and act right on time.

II. Summary of PE Courses Offered

The school regularly offers a full range of PE courses, including football, volleyball, badminton, table tennis, martial arts, shuttlecock, aerobics, and Tai Chi softball, which you can choose freely based on your interests and the difficulty level.

III. Course Venues Explained

Most PE courses are held outdoors, while a few are indoors, and the venue conditions vary significantly.

  • Shuttlecock, Table Tennis: Fully indoor courses held in the school's underground all-weather gym. The venue stays warm in winter and cool in summer; the only downside is the poor signal in the underground space;
  • Aerobics: Also an indoor course;
  • Tai Chi Softball, Martial Arts: Low-intensity movement. Although taught outdoors, they are mostly held in the shaded area of the playground with little direct sun, making for a relatively comfortable environment;
  • Football, Volleyball, Badminton: Taught in the open outdoors and heavily affected by weather — summer sun and winter wind make them tough; but these are more mainstream sports and quicker to pick up.

IV. Practical Selection Tips

  • If you want easy, comfortable classes, prioritize shuttlecock, table tennis, and aerobics — good venues and low overall pressure;
  • If you want outdoor courses with a milder environment and smaller movements, choose Tai Chi softball or martial arts;
  • Football, volleyball, and badminton involve more exercise, are taught outdoors year-round, and have higher training and assessment requirements — suited to those who love sports and are physically fit.

V. Selection Summary

Early in the semester, selection is likely offline, so prepare in advance for easier courses; later selections are all online via the academic system and require acting right on time.

Prioritize courses that suit you based on venue and difficulty, and do not blindly follow popular trends.
